### 2A. Industrial Equipment & Machinery Brokerage

**The market:** Massive and fragmented. Refineries, chemical plants, manufacturing facilities constantly buying/selling used equipment. Houston is ground zero for this.

**Commission structures:**

- **Valves** (Brandon's wheelhouse): 15-25% markup typical for distributors, 5-10% for brokers
- **Heavy machinery/construction:** 5-10% commission
- **Entire plant liquidations:** 3-8% on total deal value (but deals are $500K-$50M+)

**How to get started:**
1. **Register an LLC** (Longer Industries already exists — confirm it's suitable or create a DBA)
2. **Key platforms to list/find equipment:**
   - **Machinio.com** — largest used equipment marketplace. List for free, pay for premium placement.
   - **EquipNet.com** — used lab, processing, packaging equipment
   - **Bid on Equipment** (bidonequipment.com) — auction surplus
   - **Federal Equipment Company** — process equipment
   - **GovPlanet.com** — government surplus equipment auctions (military, industrial)
   - **IronPlanet.com** — heavy equipment
   - **Ritchie Bros** — auction house (good for sourcing cheap)
   - **LoopNet** (for when companies are liquidating entire facilities)
3. **Build relationships with:**
   - Plant turnaround companies (they know what's being decommissioned)
   - Bankruptcy attorneys (first call when a manufacturer goes under)
   - EPC firms (they need equipment for new builds, often accept used)
   - Scrap yards (sometimes have valuable equipment they don't recognize)

**Revenue potential:**

**Brandon's edge:** VP-level valve experience + manufacturing knowledge + Houston location (petrochemical capital of the US) = natural unfair advantage.

## 4. Government Contracting

### 4A. Registration Process (Do This Immediately)

**Step 1: Get a DUNS/UEI Number**
- As of April 2022, the old DUNS number was replaced by the **Unique Entity Identifier (UEI)**
- Assigned automatically when you register at SAM.gov
- **Free** — ignore anyone trying to charge you

**Step 2: Register on SAM.gov**
- Go to sam.gov → "Get Started" → "Register Entity"
- You'll need:
  - Legal business name and address
  - EIN (Employer Identification Number)
  - Bank account info (for electronic funds transfer)
  - NAICS codes (select all that apply — see below)
  - Business size info
- Registration takes **up to 10 business days** to go active
- **Must renew annually** (free)

**Relevant NAICS codes for Brandon:**
- 332992 — Small Arms Ammunition Manufacturing
- 332993 — Ammunition (Except Small Arms) Manufacturing
- 332911 — Industrial Valve Manufacturing
- 332919 — Other Metal Valve and Pipe Fitting Manufacturing
- 423840 — Industrial Supplies Merchant Wholesalers
- 423860 — Transportation Equipment and Supplies Merchant Wholesalers
- 425120 — Wholesale Trade Agents and Brokers
- 541611 — Administrative Management and General Management Consulting
- 541614 — Process, Physical Distribution, and Logistics Consulting
- 541690 — Other Scientific and Technical Consulting

**Step 3: Get a CAGE Code**
- **Commercial and Government Entity (CAGE) Code** — assigned automatically as part of SAM registration
- If you need one faster, request through DLA (dla.mil)
- International version: NCAGE code

**Step 4: Get on GSA Schedule (longer play, 3-6 months)**
- Allows you to sell directly to federal agencies
- Most relevant schedules:
  - **MAS (Multiple Award Schedule)** — catch-all for products and services
  - Category: Industrial Products and Services
- Process: Submit offer through GSA eBuy/eOffer system
- Requires: Past performance, pricing proposal, financial statements

### 4B. Contracting Strategies

**Subcontracting (fastest path):**
- Large defense primes (Lockheed, RTX, General Dynamics, Northrop) are REQUIRED to subcontract to small businesses
- Search **SubNet** (subnet.sba.gov) for subcontracting opportunities
- Reach out directly to prime contractor small business liaison officers

**8(a) / HUBZone / Other Set-Asides:**
- Brandon doesn't qualify for 8(a) (socially disadvantaged)
- Check if his zip code qualifies for **HUBZone** (Historically Underutilized Business Zones)
- **Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned** — does not apply unless veteran
- **Small Business** designation alone still gets preferential treatment on many contracts

**Where to find opportunities:**
- **SAM.gov** — search contract opportunities (formerly FedBizOpps)
- **USASpending.gov** — research what agencies are buying and from whom
- **FPDS.gov** — Federal Procurement Data System (historical contract data)
- **GovWin (Deltek)** — paid intelligence on upcoming contracts ($$$$ but worth it for serious players)
- **APEX Accelerators** (formerly PTACs — Procurement Technical Assistance Centers) — FREE counseling on government contracting. Find yours at aptac-us.org. **Houston has multiple APEX locations — go talk to them this week.**

## 5. Import/Export

### 5A. ITAR Registration (Defense Articles — Mandatory)

**What is it:** Anyone who manufactures, exports, or **brokers** defense articles or defense services must register with the Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC) at the State Department.

**Process:**
1. Register at **pmddtc.state.gov**
2. Submit form DS-2032 (Registration Statement)
3. Annual registration fee: **$2,250/year** (as of latest published rates)
4. Processing time: 30-60 days
5. Must renew annually

**What it covers:**
- Brokering ammunition (USML Category III)
- Brokering ordnance/explosives (USML Category IV)
- Brokering military vehicles (USML Category VII)
- Any defense-related middleman activity with foreign persons

**Key point:** Brandon likely already has ITAR registration from American Munitions / GCDL. **Check if it's still active.** If lapsed, re-register immediately — this is a gating item for the most lucrative opportunities.

### 5B. FFL (Federal Firearms License)

**For ammunition import specifically:**
- **Type 06 FFL** — Manufacturer of Ammunition (if manufacturing)
- **Type 08 FFL** — Importer of Firearms and Ammunition (if importing)
- **Type 11 FFL** — Dealer in Firearms (not including destructive devices)
- Processing time: 60-90 days
- Cost: $150-$200 for 3-year license

**Does Brandon need one for brokering?** Not necessarily — if he's connecting buyer and seller without taking title/possession. But if he's importing under his own name, yes. **Consult an FFL attorney.**

### 5C. EAR/Commerce Department (Dual-Use Items)

**For industrial valves, non-military equipment, dual-use technology:**
- Controlled by Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) under Export Administration Regulations (EAR)
- Many items are EAR99 (no license required for most destinations)
- Some valve types (high-pressure, high-temp, certain alloys) may require licenses for certain countries
- Severe service valves used in nuclear/chemical applications = extra scrutiny

**Process:**
- Self-classify your items using Commerce Control List (CCL)
- Apply for licenses through BIS SNAP-R system
- Screen all parties against denied persons/entities lists

### 5D. Customs Broker License (Optional but Valuable)

**What:** Licensed customs brokers can clear goods through US Customs on behalf of importers. Having your own license means you don't pay a broker for every shipment.

**Process:**
- Pass the Customs Broker License Exam (given twice yearly, April and October)
- Background check
- No degree required
- Exam is difficult (pass rate ~10-15%) but Brandon is smart enough

**Alternative:** Just partner with a good customs broker and bake their fees into your pricing.

### 5E. Import/Export Opportunities

**Industrial valves (lowest regulatory barrier):**
- Import valves from India, China, South Korea, Italy for US distribution
- Houston has massive demand from petrochemical sector
- Margins: 30-60% on imported valves vs domestic
- No defense/ITAR issues (unless nuclear-grade)
- **This could be a $1M+/yr business within 18 months**

**Ammunition import:**
- Brandon already knows this business
- Eastern European manufacturers (Serbia, Czech Republic, Romania, Bulgaria) produce quality ammo at 40-60% of US cost
- US market demand remains strong
- Requires: FFL Type 08, ITAR registration, import permits from ATF (Form 6)
- **Could restart this with existing contacts, lower overhead (brokerage not manufacturing)**

**Defense equipment/ordnance (highest margin, highest barrier):**
- This is where the $1M+/yr deals happen
- Requires: ITAR registration, end-user certificates, State Department DSP-5 or DSP-73 licenses
- Brandon has the contacts and experience — this is his differentiator
